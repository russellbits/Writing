💡 What are Watts?

A watt (W) is the standard unit of power in the International System of Units (SI).

Definition: Power is the rate at which energy is used or produced.

Analogy: If energy is the distance you travel, power is your speed.

Electrical Context: One watt is equal to one joule of energy per second. In an electrical circuit, it's defined as one ampere of current flowing under the pressure of one volt (W=A×V).


Namesake: The unit is named after James Watt, a Scottish inventor known for his improvements to the steam engine.

Larger amounts of power are often measured in:

Kilowatts (kW): 1,000 watts

Megawatts (MW): 1,000,000 watts

🧠 How Many Watts Does the Human Brain Use?

The human brain is remarkably energy-efficient. It is consistently estimated to use about 20 watts of power.


This is roughly equivalent to the power draw of a dim, old-fashioned incandescent lightbulb or a modern computer monitor in sleep mode.

Despite this low power consumption, the brain performs an immense number of operations, leading researchers to study its structure for developing more energy-efficient AI.
