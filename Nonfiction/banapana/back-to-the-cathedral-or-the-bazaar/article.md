# Back to the Cathedral and the Bazaar, Part Trés

The way I see it, in the 1970s we had a big struggle between the Cathedral (Corporations selling mainframes to other corporations) and the Bazaar (hobbyists and personal computer builder clubs doing it for themselves). With the advent of the Internet, this dynamic presented itself again only then it was closed source software (The Cathedral, a la software companies) and open source software (The Bazaar). Sometimes the people in the Bazaar actually build Cathedrals out of their DIY clubs.
