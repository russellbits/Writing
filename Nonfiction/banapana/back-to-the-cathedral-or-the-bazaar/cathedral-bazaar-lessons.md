#Lessons

Every good work of software starts by scratching a developer’s personal itch

Good programmers know what to write. Great programmers know what to rewrite (and reuse)

So if you want to get it right, be ready to start over at least once

When you lose interest in a program, your last duty is to hand it off to a competent successor

Treating your users as your co-developers is your least hassle route to rapid code improvement and effective debugging

Release early. Release often. And listen to your customers.

‘’Somebody finds the problem’’ he says, ‘’and somebody else understands it’’

Any tool should be useful in the expected way, but a truly great tool lends itself to uses you never expected

But if you’re writing for the world, you have to listen to your customers - this doesn’t change just because they are not paying you in money

To solve an interesting problem start by finding a problem that is interesting to you

Joy is an asset

Enjoyment predicts efficiency

Software is largely a service industry operating under the persistent but unfounded delusion that it is a manufacturing industry

They copied all they could follow,
but they couldn’t copy my mind,
And I let’em sweating and stealing
a year and a half behind.

Hackers solve problems and build things, and they believe in freedom and voluntary mutual help.

The world is full of fascinating problems waiting to be solved.
