Enhitifcation-synopsis.md

Cory Doctorow’s “enshittification” argument is that online platforms tend to degrade in a predictable pattern: they first attract users with a good experience, then shift value toward business customers or advertisers, and finally squeeze both sides to maximize profit. He says the main cause is not just bad leadership or “evil” personalities, but a policy and market environment that removes discipline from platform companies—especially weak antitrust enforcement, regulatory capture, and lock-in that makes it hard for users to leave. [bylinesupplement](https://www.bylinesupplement.com/p/engineered-estrangement-an-interview)

## Core idea

Doctorow’s basic claim is that platforms become worse over time because they are optimized to extract more value once they have control over both users and business partners. In his framing, the company’s incentives change after it has captured the market, so the service is progressively hollowed out. [cybernews](https://cybernews.com/news/cory-doctorow-enshittification-book-review/)

## What he says causes it

The cause, as Doctorow describes it, is a lack of competitive pressure and public oversight. He argues that features like interoperability, user exit options, and strong antitrust rules used to discipline companies, and when those constraints weaken, platforms can steadily degrade services without losing enough customers to stop them. [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=h3A2VNsjEns)

## Simple example

A platform may start by serving posts in a user-friendly order, then begin adding ads and sponsored content, and later make the experience much worse for users and creators alike while taking a bigger cut for itself. That pattern is what Doctorow calls enshittification. [reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/BreadTube/comments/16m5xxe/cory_doctorow_explains_how_the_enshitification_of/)
