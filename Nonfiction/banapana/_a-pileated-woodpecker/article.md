# A Pileated Woodpecker

I often describe consciousness as simply what it is like to exist in a higher dimensional space than three or four. This is nonsensical to most people, so I describe it with a moment in time: I was walking through the woods and I saw, to my shock, a pileated woodpecker—an unusual and hard to find bird. What that moments was an intersection of mutually exclusive perception that exist on the own dimensions. Colors are a dimension or place, the pileated woodpecker having a large red crest is helpful. Color is intersected by lines of contrast outlining the form and separating it from the tree. [Numerosity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Numerosity_adaptation_effect) tells me that there's just the one bird. Information can enter into the equation. So what does it mean that my breath quickened and my heart rate rose?
