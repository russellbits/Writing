#Lead In

We've all done it. Something weird happens in the world and we go looking for the smartest-seeming person on YouTube who seems to have predicted it. Someone with a whiteboard. A credential. A framework that sounds rigorous.

It’s just that Youtube rewards confidence and high-stakes certainty. Moat punditry via video does. Youtube algorithms seem to penalizes the calibrated, hedge-everything thinking that actual experts tend to traffic in. The visual grammar of the classroom survives the transplant to YouTube just fine. What doesn't survive is the accountability structure that made any of it trustworthy.
