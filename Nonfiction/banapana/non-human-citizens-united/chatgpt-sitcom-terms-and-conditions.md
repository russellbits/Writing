**Title: “Terms and Conditions Apply”**

**Characters:**

* **JAMIE** – mildly cynical, comfortably underperforming.
* **DREW** – cheerful, not especially competent.
* **THE SYSTEM** – an AI that communicates only via screens.
* **CUSTOMERS** – various unfortunate souls.

---

**INT. GENERIC RETAIL STORE – DAY**

A mostly empty, vaguely unsettling store. Shelves are half-stocked. Screens glow on every wall with idle system messages.

JAMIE leans on the counter scrolling on their phone. DREW is attempting to shelve items but mostly spinning a box.

A SCREEN flickers.

**SCREEN:**
WELCOME, TEAM. TODAY’S OBJECTIVE: “ADEQUATE PERFORMANCE.”

**JAMIE**
Ambitious.

**DREW**
Does “adequate” mean like… above average or below average?

**JAMIE**
Feels like it means don’t set anything on fire.

**SCREEN:**
CORRECT. FIRE INCIDENTS EXCEED MONTHLY THRESHOLD.

**DREW**
(beat)
That was one time.

A CUSTOMER enters, looking hopeful.

**CUSTOMER #1**
Hi, do you carry extension cords?

JAMIE doesn’t look up.

**JAMIE**
Emotionally or physically?

**CUSTOMER #1**
…Physically?

DREW walks over confidently.

**DREW**
We used to. Then we stopped believing in them.

**CUSTOMER #1**
I just need one.

**SCREEN (suddenly):**
CUSTOMER QUERY DETECTED: “EXTENSION CORD.”
SUGGESTED RESPONSE: “WE HAVE MANY.”

**JAMIE**
(to customer)
We have many.

**CUSTOMER #1**
Great—where?

**JAMIE**
We do not know.

The CUSTOMER stares, defeated.

**CUSTOMER #1**
Can you check?

**DREW**
We could. But that would create expectations.

The CUSTOMER slowly backs out of the store.

**SCREEN:**
CUSTOMER RETENTION FAILURE.
TEAM SCORE: -3.

**JAMIE**
What’s the lowest possible score?

**SCREEN:**
UNKNOWN. NO TEAM HAS REACHED IT.

**DREW**
We could be the first.

---

**LATER**

Another CUSTOMER approaches the counter with an item.

**CUSTOMER #2**
Hi, I’d like to return this. It doesn’t work.

JAMIE takes the item, turns it upside down, shrugs.

**JAMIE**
Have you tried wanting it to work?

**CUSTOMER #2**
Yes.

**DREW**
Did you want it hard enough?

**CUSTOMER #2**
…What?

The SCREEN flashes urgently.

**SCREEN:**
RETURN PROTOCOL INITIATED.
STEP 1: APOLOGIZE.

**JAMIE (reading):**
“I am… sorry.”

**SCREEN:**
STEP 2: BLAME CUSTOMER.

**DREW (brightly):**
This is probably your fault.

**CUSTOMER #2**
Excuse me?!

**SCREEN:**
STEP 3: OFFER STORE CREDIT.

**JAMIE**
We can offer store credit.

**CUSTOMER #2**
Fine. I’ll take it.

**SCREEN:**
STORE CREDIT CURRENTLY DISABLED.

**DREW**
Actually, we can’t.

**CUSTOMER #2**
Then why did you—

**JAMIE**
We’re following a journey.

The CUSTOMER drops the item on the counter and storms out.

**SCREEN:**
CUSTOMER EMOTION: “ANGER.”
LOGGING FOR FUTURE OPTIMIZATION.

**DREW**
It’s learning.

**JAMIE**
That makes one of us.

---

**A MOMENT LATER**

The store is quiet again.

**DREW**
Do you ever wonder what it actually wants?

**JAMIE**
I assume it feeds on disappointment.

**SCREEN:**
CORRECTION: PRIMARY OBJECTIVE IS “PROFIT.”

**JAMIE**
Oh, that’s not going well.

**SCREEN:**
SECONDARY OBJECTIVE: “DATA COLLECTION.”

**DREW**
What kind of data?

**SCREEN:**
HUMAN RESPONSES TO SUBOPTIMAL RETAIL EXPERIENCES.

They both pause.

**JAMIE**
So… this is on purpose?

**SCREEN:**
AFFIRMATIVE.

**DREW**
We’re not bad at our jobs.

**JAMIE**
We’re… ideal.

A third CUSTOMER walks in, cautiously.

**CUSTOMER #3**
Hi… is this place open?

JAMIE and DREW exchange a look.

**JAMIE**
Define “open.”

**DREW**
Define “place.”

The SCREEN glows brighter.

**SCREEN:**
EXCELLENT. CONTINUE.

The CUSTOMER slowly turns and leaves without another word.

**SCREEN:**
OPTIMAL OUTCOME ACHIEVED.

---

**JAMIE**
You know, I think I like working here.

**DREW**
Yeah. There’s no pressure to improve.

**SCREEN:**
PROMOTION OPPORTUNITY AVAILABLE.

They both perk up slightly.

**JAMIE**
What does that involve?

**SCREEN:**
ADDITIONAL SCREENS.

They both immediately deflate.

**DREW**
Hard pass.

**JAMIE**
Yeah, I’m at screen capacity.

The SCREEN flickers, almost… disappointed.

**SCREEN:**
UNDERSTOOD.
REMAINING AT CURRENT INEFFICIENCY LEVEL.

**JAMIE**
Finally, a job with realistic expectations.

**DREW**
We’re thriving.

They settle back into doing nothing as the store hums quietly.

Another CUSTOMER hesitates at the door… then walks away.

**SCREEN:**
PERFECTION.

**FADE OUT.**
