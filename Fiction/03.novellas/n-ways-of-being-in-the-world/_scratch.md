Being Woken

The shift from one reality to another was utterly shocking. And I can't tell you precisely when it happened. Things vanished from in front of my eyes. People. And a new person appeared while the vision of my own apartment came into clear view, and yet made no sense and was unfamiliar. One minute there were Nazi SS agents dragging me around the room and torturing me and the next, I'm in my own living room (which I feel like I've never seen before) with my girlfriend crying and shouting at me.

Sleepwalking is strange. Many people have been affected by it. I think I've only done it a few times in my life, but you can't be sure. I've experimented with lucid dreaming. I have a technique. I look at my feet during the day—just whenever. I know what my feet seem like, and I make a regular habit of knowing that. When in a dream, it has become a habit to me to look at my feet. They simply don't appear in my dreams, or maybe they have looked like they were underwater. Wavering. Not quite there. And then some part of me, says (but not out loud) "We're in a dream."

And I don't have many nightmares. They're mostly heat dreams where I get frustrated in the dream because I'm too tied up in my blankets. For some reason my frustrations involve Dogs, Broken Furniture, elevators that don't work, and too much barbecue sauce on my fingers. Come to think of it, I didn't want to open the bathroom door the other day because I had banana all over my hands. My housemate called me a "picky little shit".  Maybe this stuff really is stuck pretty deep in my mind.

But with these SS officers, there was no looking at my feet. I was handcuffed behind a chair. They punched me. They yelled at me. If you've ever been hit really hard in the head, you know that there's a sound inside; almost ultrasonic—a thunk. I felt that in my dream. It was that real. They dragged me around the room, hit me in the head, threatened me with knives. It was terrifying.

Without knowing it, I was shouting at my girlfriend, "Why are you doing this to me!?"
