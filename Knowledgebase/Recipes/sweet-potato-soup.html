Turmeric & Coconut Milk
Sweet Potato Soup

A savory, earthy soup that plays turmeric's bitterness against coconut milk's richness. The aromatics base does the heavy lifting — the sweet potato almost disappears into something more interesting than itself.

Prep 15 min
Cook 35 min
Total 50 min
Serves 4

Ingredients

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes (~700g), peeled and cut into 2cm chunks
  • 1 can (400ml) full-fat coconut milk
  • 500ml vegetable broth
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated (about a 3cm piece)
  • 1½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground coriander
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper, or to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt, plus more to taste
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Toasted pumpkin seeds, to serve
  • Fresh cilantro or sliced scallions, to serve
  • Black pepper, to taste

Method

  1. 01

    Build the aromatics base

    Heat olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook, stirring occasionally, for 7–8 minutes until soft and translucent. Don't rush this — the onion is the flavor foundation.

  2. 02

    Bloom the spices

    Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and cook for 2 minutes until fragrant. Add the turmeric, cumin, coriander, and cayenne. Stir constantly for 60 seconds — you're blooming the spices in the oil, which deepens their flavor significantly.

  3. 03

    Add sweet potato and liquids

    Add the sweet potato chunks and stir to coat in the spiced oil. Pour in the vegetable broth and coconut milk. Add 1 teaspoon of salt and stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.

  4. 04

    Simmer until tender

    Simmer uncovered for 20–25 minutes, until the sweet potato is completely tender and breaks apart easily when pressed with a spoon.

  5. 05

    Blend

    Use an immersion blender to blend directly in the pot until smooth. For a chunkier texture, blend only half and stir it back in. Adjust consistency with a splash of broth if needed.

  6. 06

    Finish and taste

    Squeeze in the juice of one lime and stir. Taste and adjust salt, cayenne, and lime. The lime is not optional — it cuts through the sweetness and lifts the whole soup.

  7. 07

    Serve

    Ladle into bowls. Top with toasted pumpkin seeds, fresh cilantro or sliced scallions, a crack of black pepper, and optionally a small drizzle of coconut cream or a few drops of chili oil.

Cook's notes

  • Full-fat coconut milk matters here — the fat carries the spices and adds body. Light coconut milk will produce a thin, watery result.
  • Fresh ginger is significantly better than ground in this recipe. If you only have ground, use ½ tsp.
  • The lime at the end is the single most important finishing move. Don't skip it.
  • If you want more heat, add a fresh red chili with the garlic instead of relying on cayenne alone.
  • Leftovers thicken considerably overnight. Thin with broth when reheating.
  • Toasted pumpkin seeds add a satisfying crunch and a subtle nuttiness that pairs very well with the turmeric.
